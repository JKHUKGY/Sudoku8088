# Writing Your Resume Summary with AI

## Objective

Learn to use AI tools to write targeted Resume Summaries. Master the `/summary-writer` and `/summary-reviewer` commands, develop an iterative write-review workflow, and produce multiple Summary versions for different job directions.

Read the tutorial: [Build Your Resume with AI - Lesson 05](https://github.com/easyscale-academy/learn_build_resume-project/tree/05-Summary)

## Actionable Items

1. Prepare your `profile/` directory with detailed background information (one bullet per project, expanded work experience)
2. Use `/summary-writer` to draft your first Summary
3. Use `/summary-reviewer` to evaluate it against a target Job Description
4. Iterate at least 3 rounds between writing and reviewing
5. Submit your final Summary with supporting materials for instructor review
6. After instructor confirmation, create 3 additional Summaries with different positioning

**Estimated time:** 45-60 minutes

## Checklist

- [ ] **Profile prepared** - Background information in `profile/` directory with detailed, itemized content
- [ ] **First draft complete** - Used `/summary-writer` to generate initial Summary
- [ ] **JD selected** - Chose a target Job Description and saved to `application/job-description.md`
- [ ] **Review completed** - Used `/summary-reviewer` to evaluate Summary against JD
- [ ] **3+ iterations done** - Went through at least 3 write-review cycles
- [ ] **Submission ready** - Prepared Summary + Resume + JD + Reviewer feedback for instructor
- [ ] **Multiple versions** - Created 3 Summaries with different positioning (after instructor confirmation)

## Submission & Verification

When you've completed at least 3 iterations, submit the following in the course card comments:

1. Your final Summary
2. Your resume or profile content
3. The Job Description you targeted
4. The `/summary-reviewer` evaluation from your final round

After instructor review and confirmation, continue to create 3 Summaries with different positioning angles. Save them in `resume/summary/` with descriptive filenames.

## Grading Rubric

- **Profile quality:** Student has detailed, itemized background information (not just a rough resume dump)
- **Tool usage:** Student demonstrates correct use of both `/summary-writer` and `/summary-reviewer` commands
- **Iteration evidence:** Submission shows at least 3 rounds of revision based on reviewer feedback
- **Summary quality:** Final Summary is specific, credible, and aligned with target JD (not generic buzzwords)
- **Positioning variety:** Student's 3 Summaries show genuinely different angles (not just minor wording changes)
- **Length appropriateness:** Summaries are concise (2-3 lines) and would fit on an actual resume
